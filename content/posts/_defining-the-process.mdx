<ul>
<li><p><strong>Concept</strong> &#8211; Clearly define the purpose of the site. Who is the audience? What is the character, message, feel?</p></li>
<li><p><strong>Content</strong> &#8211; What type of information is being conveyed and how. How will the data be structured, stored, and accessed? How will it be organized, tagged, and navigated?</p></li>
<li><p><strong>Wireframing</strong> &#8211; Develop the layout of the site. Determine what types of pages there will be: feature, index, directory, form, search. Create page elements: header, footer, sidebar, navigation.</p></li>
<li><p><strong>Visuals</strong> &#8211; What is this thing going to look like? Decide on colors, fonts, imagery. How does the concept influence the style? Is there a feeling or mood you are trying to create?</p></li>
<li><p><strong>Behavior</strong> &#8211; How is the site navigated? How does the user interact with the site? Are there messages, forms, and feedback? Is the there motion, transitions, animations?</p></li>
<li><p><strong>Tools</strong> &#8211; What is the server and database setup? Generate HTML, CSS, and scripts. What is the content management system? Setup your URLs, directory structure, and development environment.</p></li>
<li><p><strong>Deploy</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Feedback</strong> &#8211; How is the site being used? Study the analytics data. Is the site achieving its purpose?</p></li>
<li><p><strong>Iterate</strong></p></li>
</ul>
